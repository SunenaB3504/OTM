Introduction to Multiplication

Multiplication as Repeated Addition

What is Multiplication?

Multiplication is a faster way to add the same number multiple times. When we multiply, we're adding a number over and over again.

Step 1: Let's Use Objects We Know

We can use familiar objects like blocks, toy cars, or candies to learn multiplication.

For example, let's use blocks to understand multiplication.

Step 2: Starting Small

Let's begin with small numbers like 2 × 3 (read as "two times three").

Step 4: Let's Say It Together

When we see 2 × 3, we can say:

  • "2 groups of 3"
  • "3 added 2 times"
  • "3 plus another 3"

Step 5: The Multiplication Symbol (×)

The "×" symbol means we're doing multiplication.

When we write 2 × 3 = 6, it means the same as 3 + 3 = 6.

When we write 3 × 4 = 12, it means the same as 4 + 4 + 4 = 12.

Step 7: Real-Life Examples

Example 1: Friends with Stickers

If 3 friends each have 4 stickers, how many stickers are there in total?

That's 3 groups of 4 stickers.

4 + 4 + 4 = 12
So, 3 × 4 = 12 stickers in total

Example 2: Car Wheels

Each car has 4 wheels. How many wheels do 2 cars have?

That's 2 groups of 4 wheels.

4 + 4 = 8
So, 2 × 4 = 8 wheels in total

Example 3: Dog Legs

Each dog has 4 legs. How many legs do 3 dogs have?

That's 3 groups of 4 legs.

4 + 4 + 4 = 12
So, 3 × 4 = 12 legs in total

Try these multiplication facts:

Step 10: Let's Check What We Learned

Questions to answer:

1. What does multiplication mean?

Multiplication is adding the same number many times.

2. What does 4 × 3 mean?

It means 4 groups of 3 or adding 3 four times (3 + 3 + 3 + 3).

3. If I have 3 baskets with 6 apples in each, how many apples do I have total?

3 × 6 = 6 + 6 + 6 = 18 apples

Let's Create Your Own Example!

Think of your own multiplication problem using things around you.

For example: "If I have 2 hands and each hand has 5 fingers, how many fingers total?"

Answer: 2 × 5 = 10 fingers
