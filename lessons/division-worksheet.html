Basic Division Problems

Solve these basic division problems. Type your answer in the box.

Problem 1
8 ÷ 2 =
Think about how many groups of 2 you can make from 8 objects.
Problem 2
15 ÷ 3 =
If you have 15 objects and put them in groups of 3, how many groups will you have?
Problem 3
20 ÷ 4 =
Think about the multiplication fact: 5 × 4 = 20. So 20 ÷ 4 must be 5!
Problem 4
9 ÷ 3 =
Think about the multiplication fact: 3 × 3 = 9. So 9 ÷ 3 must be 3!
Problem 5
16 ÷ 4 =

Problem 6
25 ÷ 5 =

Problem 7

Problem 8
0 ÷ 5 =

Problem 9
12 ÷ 1 =
When you divide a number by 1, you get the same number back. So 12 ÷ 1 = 12.
Problem 10

Division with Remainders

Solve these division problems. Some might have remainders!

Problem 11
7 ÷ 2 = with remainder
How many complete groups of 2 can you make with 7 objects? How many objects will be left over?
Problem 12
11 ÷ 3 = with remainder
3 × 3 = 9, and 11 - 9 = 2. So you can make 3 groups of 3 with 2 left over.
Problem 13
17 ÷ 5 = with remainder
5 × 3 = 15, and 17 - 15 = 2. So you can make 3 groups of 5 with 2 left over.

Word Problems

Solve these division word problems.

Problem 14

Maria has 24 cookies. She wants to share them equally among 6 friends. How many cookies will each friend get?

Each friend gets cookies.

To solve this problem, divide the total number of cookies by the number of friends: 24 ÷ 6.
Problem 15

Sam has 30 stickers. He wants to put them on 4 pages, with the same number of stickers on each page. How many stickers will be on each page? Will there be any stickers left over?

Each page will have stickers with stickers left over.

Divide 30 by 4. Remember to check if there's a remainder by multiplying your answer by 4 and comparing to 30.
Problem 16

A teacher has 27 students in her class. She wants to arrange them into equal groups for a project. If she wants 3 students in each group, how many groups will she need?

She will need groups.

To find the number of groups, divide the total number of students by the number of students in each group: 27 ÷ 3.

Problem 18

A baker has 100 muffins. She puts them in boxes of 12. How many full boxes can she make, and how many muffins will be left over?

She can make full boxes with muffins left over.

Divide 100 by 12. How many times does 12 go into 100? Then find how many are left over.
