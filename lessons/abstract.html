Abstract Learning

Working with Numbers and Symbols

What is Abstract Learning?

Abstract learning is the final stage in understanding math concepts. At this stage, we work directly with numbers and symbols, without needing physical objects or pictures to help us.

The Connection Between Multiplication and Division

Remember, multiplication and division are related operations. We can use this connection to solve problems.

Using Related Facts

If we know that 6 √ó 7 = 42, then we also know:

  • 7 √ó 6 = 42
  • 42 √∑ 7 = 6
  • 42 √∑ 6 = 7

This is called a fact family.

Mental Math Strategies

At the abstract level, we can also develop mental math strategies to solve problems quickly.

Using Known Facts

If you know that 5 √ó 6 = 30, you can find 6 √ó 6 by adding one more group of 6:

6 √ó 6 = (5 √ó 6) + 6 = 30 + 6 = 36

Using Multiples of 10

Multiplying by 10 is easy - just add a zero. You can use this to help with other multiplications:

7 √ó 9 = (7 √ó 10) - 7 = 70 - 7 = 63
